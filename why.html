From the founder

Why H.I.M. exists

H.I.M. started with a simple observation: many of the tools that once made online interaction feel personal have been replaced by products optimized for speed and surface-level engagement.

I wanted to build something that felt more human.

Who I am

I'm Haaris, and H.I.M. is my attempt to create a social app for gay men that gives more weight to personality, conversation, and community than the current category typically allows.

The problem

Most apps built for gay men are built around the same assumptions. Photos first. Proximity first. Quick decisions.

That works for hookups, but it leaves very little room for friendship.

That gap feels especially clear for gay men. Some apps are hookup-first by design. Some are still dating-adjacent. Others are broader queer social platforms, but they do not feel specifically built for gay men in a focused way.

What H.I.M. does differently

H.I.M. is meant to solve for that gap.

It draws from what made AIM feel personal and social: screennames, away messages, buddy lists, and chat rooms.

Not just for nostalgia, but because those mechanics made more room for identity, regular interaction, and actual community.

H.I.M. is not a dating app in disguise. It is not another hookup app with softer branding. It is meant to be a real social product in its own right.
