This is the full origin story. Use it for press, interviews, the about page, or anywhere you need to explain where H.I.M. came from in your own words.

H.I.M. started as a joke. My best friend and I were messing around with the idea of bringing back AIM. The original name was HIS I.M. Just a riff, something that made us laugh. But I kept thinking about it. HIS felt too singular, too possessive. What I actually wanted was something collective, something that belonged to all of us. So HIS became Hi, It's Me. And once I had that, I couldn't let it go. I'm a single, divorced gay man in my mid-thirties. I work hard, I have a full life on paper, and I spend a lot of time alone on my couch trying to figure out how to actually connect with people. Not hook up. Not perform for a bar crowd. Just connect. Make friends. Build something real. Every time I opened an app looking for that, I got a grid of faces sorted by how close they were to me. I got a product that was built to get me to swipe, not to get me to stay. I wasn't looking for what those apps were selling. But there was nothing else. So I built something myself. I kept coming back to AIM, to what it actually felt like to be online in 2002. You had a screenname that you chose, that meant something. You had an away message that was basically your diary, your mood, your proof that you were a real person. You had a buddy list that reflected your actual relationships, not an algorithm's guess at what might keep you scrolling. That wasn't just nostalgia. That was a better social contract than anything we have now. H.I.M. is built for gay men who remember what real connection felt like online, and who are tired of being told that a grid of strangers sorted by proximity is the best we can do. I couldn't find the app I needed. So I built it.

About page: full text
H.I.M. started as a joke. My best friend and I were messing around with the idea of rebuilding AIM. The original name was HIS I.M., just a riff between two people who apparently never got over the sound of a door creaking at 11pm. But I kept thinking about it, and HIS felt too narrow. What I actually wanted was something collective. So HIS became Hi, It's Me. I'm Haaris. I'm a gay man in my mid-thirties, divorced, living in Orlando, spending more time on my couch than I'd like to admit. I work in tech. I cook a lot of desi food. And for a while now I've been looking for a social app that was built for someone like me, someone who wants genuine friendship and real connection outside of the bar scene and hookup culture, and keeps coming up empty. Every app I found was built around the wrong thing. Proximity. Swipes. Faces in a grid. None of them were designed for friendship. And when I tried to use them that way, the product fought me. So I built something myself. H.I.M. is built around the AIM mechanic because that social contract was actually better than what we have now. You chose a screenname that meant something. You wrote an away message that was basically your personality on display. You had a buddy list that reflected real relationships. There was no algorithm deciding who you should talk to. You just showed up, and so did your people. I built H.I.M. for gay men who remember what that felt like. And for gay men who never experienced it but deserve something better than what's out there now. This isn't a hookup app. It's not a dating app. It's a place to be a person. Haaris
